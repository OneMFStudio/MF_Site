Hybrid Lubbock Ravine Retreat Svalbard

HYBRID

In the second-year studio project at Texas Tech University, under the guidance of Lucas Neal Hitch and coordinated by Nate Imai, I embarked on a challenging and innovative endeavor. Inspired by two distinct architectural precedents, the Small House in Tokyo and the Yokohama Port Terminal, I undertook the creation of a hybrid model that synthesized elements from both sources.

Through a detailed study of these precedents, I identified key components and design principles, extracting them to form a cohesive architectural system. This synthesis culminated in the development of a 15’-0” x 150’-0” model, serving as a prototype for future habitation. The primary objective of this model was to establish a scalable unit that could be replicated and expanded upon in subsequent phases of the semester.

In addition to constructing the physical model, I was tasked with generating a series of diagrams and drawings to further elucidate the design concept. These visual representations encompassed circulation patterns, formal massing ideas, and the underlying tectonic system, providing a comprehensive overview of the project's architectural intent..
